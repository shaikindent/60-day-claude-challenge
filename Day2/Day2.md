# Section 1: Explanation

**What Prompt Engineering is**

Prompt engineering is simply the skill of writing clear, specific instructions so an AI tool understands exactly what you want and gives you a response that actually fits your needs. Think of it like giving directions to a new colleague: if you say "make something nice," you'll get something generic. If you say exactly what you need, who it's for, and how it should look, you'll get something useful on the first try.

**Why it matters when using AI tools like Claude**

Claude doesn't read your mind, it reads your words. It responds based entirely on the context, detail, and structure you give it. A vague request forces Claude to guess at your intent, which usually produces a vague, one-size-fits-all answer. A well-crafted prompt gives Claude the role, the audience, the format, and the goal, so it can produce something tailored and ready to use rather than something you have to heavily rework.

**Three practical benefits of writing better prompts**

- Saves time: fewer rounds of "no, not like that" back-and-forth, because you get closer to the right answer on the first attempt.
- Better accuracy and relevance: the output actually fits your context, audience, and purpose instead of being generic.
- More control over tone, structure, and format: you can get content that's ready to publish, present, or send, exactly the way you need it.

# Section 2: Weak vs Improved Prompt Example

**Weak prompt:**
"Write about prompt engineering."

This gives Claude almost nothing to work with: no audience, no length, no tone, no purpose. The result will likely be a generic, textbook-style explanation that could apply to anyone, anywhere.

**Improved prompt:**
"Act as an AI educator teaching complete beginners. Write a simple, friendly explanation of prompt engineering for a LinkedIn audience of professionals new to AI. Include a definition, why it matters when using Claude, one weak vs improved prompt example, and three practical benefits. Keep it under 200 words and end with a soft call-to-action inviting readers to follow my '60 Days Claude Challenge.'"

This version tells Claude the role to play, the audience, the platform, the exact content to include, the length, and the desired ending. That specificity is what turns a generic answer into a usable, on-brand piece of content.
